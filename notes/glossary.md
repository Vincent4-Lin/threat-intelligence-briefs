# Glossary

## Threat Intelligence

Information used to understand threats, attacker behavior, risk, and possible defenses.

## Indicator of Compromise

A specific artifact that may suggest compromise, such as an IP address, domain, file hash, URL, or email address.

## TTP

Tactics, techniques, and procedures. This describes how attackers operate, not just what tools they use.

## Phishing

A social engineering attack that uses email, messages, websites, or voice calls to trick users into giving information or taking unsafe actions.

## Credential Theft

Stealing usernames, passwords, tokens, MFA codes, or session information.

## Account Takeover

Unauthorized control of a legitimate user account.

## Valid Accounts

Legitimate accounts used by attackers after credentials are stolen or obtained.

## MFA

Multi-factor authentication. A login control that requires more than one proof of identity.

## Phishing-Resistant MFA

MFA designed to resist phishing, such as FIDO2/WebAuthn security keys.
